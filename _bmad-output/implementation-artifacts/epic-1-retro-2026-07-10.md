## Epic Summary

- No previous retrospective exists (Epic 1 is the project's first epic)

## What Went Well

1. **Dependency-vs-docs drift caught early and propagated forward.** Story 1.1 discovered Prisma 7 dropped the `url = env(...)` syntax the Architecture Spine's schema depends on, downgraded to 5.22, and every subsequent story explicitly noted "do not upgrade Prisma" in its Dev Notes. Story 1.3 hit an analogous NextAuth v5 beta surprise (`next-auth/jwt` module augmentation silently fails to merge because pnpm doesn't hoist `@auth/core`), documented the workaround precisely enough that Story 1.4's dev agent verified empirically rather than assumed when it hit its own beta quirk (`CredentialsSignin.code` surfacing on a separate field from `error`).
2. **"Previous Story Intelligence" habit.** Every story file carries a section passing forward exactly what the next dev agent needs to know — this measurably prevented rediscovery of the same landmines across all 5 stories.
3. **Deliberate deferral discipline.** Every story's review findings split cleanly into Patch (fixed immediately) vs. Defer (logged to `deferred-work.md` with an explicit reason). Judgment calls (Story 1.2's `ClassMembership` uniqueness scope, Story 1.3's cookie-signature reconciliation, Story 1.5's AC-vs-shipped-breakpoint wording) were flagged in Completion Notes rather than silently resolved.
4. **Real infra surprises root-caused, not papered over.** Story 1.2 hit a genuine DNS/IPv6 dead-end on the direct Supabase host mid-migration and root-caused it to a documented Supavisor pooler workaround.

## Challenges / Growth Areas

1. **No automated test framework exists anywhere in the repo.** All verification across 5 stories has been `pnpm build` clean + manual smoke test. This becomes a real blocker at Story 3.1 (adaptive difficulty), which has an explicit AC requiring 10+ unit tests.
2. **Technical debt is accumulating in `deferred-work.md`** — 25 items logged across 4 stories. None block Epic 1, but several are pre-assigned to specific future stories (Story 3.2, 7.5) and need to actually be picked up when those stories are scoped, not forgotten.
3. **Sprint-status epic flag was stale** — `epic-1` remained `in-progress` even after all 5 stories reached `done`; no automatic transition exists for epic-level completion.
